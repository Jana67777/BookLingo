The Ugly Duckling

It was so beautiful out in the country. It was summer. The wheat fields were golden, the oats were green, and down among the green meadows the hay was stacked. There the stork stalked about on his long, red legs, and chattered Egyptian, a language he had learned from his mother.

All around the fields and meadows were great forests, and in the midst of the forests lay deep lakes. Yes, it was really beautiful out in the country. Bathed in sunshine stood an old manor house, with a deep moat around it, and from the walls down to the water's edge grew great burdock leaves, so high that a little child could stand upright under the biggest of them.

It was as wild a place as the thickest wood, and here sat a duck upon her nest. She had to hatch her ducklings, but she was almost tired out, for it took such a long time, and she rarely had visitors. The other ducks preferred to swim about in the moat rather than waddle up and sit under a burdock leaf to gossip with her.

At last one eggshell after another burst open. "Cheep! cheep!" they cried. All the yolks had come to life, and were sticking out their heads.
